Temporary bio...

As far I can remember, I was fascinated with all that electronics and computers mean. I was a curious person from an early age. I used to dismantle and dissect a product and often I wouldn't stop until I knew what would make the device tick. My first “real” taste of computers came when my mother gifted me a laptop for my 4th birthday. It was an overkill laptop for a kid of my age and the poor thing got hammered. On good old windows XP, I used to verify the function of every button, switch and checkbox to the point that every couple of weeks I needed to send my laptop to a computer service because of his OS being so dysfunctional, most of the times, couldn’t even boot properly in the OS. In conclusion, that was the beginning of my “great journey” into the world of software.

I like games a lot and I enjoy analysing all the little details, especially in regards to UI/UX. Two years ago, I started making my own projects. That might have been a little late, bearing in my mind that I’ve been interested in programming all my life. I started creating small web design concepts which I further continued to improve and experiment with.

One of my hobby is to code. I started in with web design because it’s the most accessible way to make an UI concept. Using the skills learnt, I created my own website: simonedifonzo.com. Project that I started at the beginning of October this year. On the website, there are some of my past projects in which I used to experiment with different design ideas.
